Sai Kiran Kothuri & Meena Kamal Gella's

Marriage

In 2021 on June 17th, Sai Kiran Kothuri & Meena Kamal Gella got married in Fremont, California.
This website is dedicated to their beautiful journey into their next chapter of life together.

Monday, June 14th 2021

Haldi

The first event was to make Meena officially a bride. This meant applying haldi to her face and her maid in honor / little sister, Sumadhura and
then pouring water at the end. This was done at our home in San Jose by their parents and ours and a few close family friends.


Later, lots of good food was served for lunch!

Mehendi

The next event was the Mehendi event where hands were drawn on with mehendi cones to create special traditional patterns
followed by snacks laid out for the guests to enjoy while they wait for their turn.

Thursday, June 17th 2021

At last, the wedding has arrived!
All rituals are shown below step-by-step.

Gauri Pooja

The bride offers her prayers to the goddess Gauri for a similar ideal relationship that the goddess has with her husband, Lord Shiva.

Ganesh Pooja

All the auspicious occasions begin with Ganesh Pooja in Hindi religion since Lord Ganesha is believed to remove any obstacles in the way. Hence, Ganesh Pooja is the first ritual that happens at the "mandapam" stage.

Vadhu Pravesam

The bride enters the "mandapam" (stage). Until the "muhurtam" (auspicious time), the bride and groom are not supposed to look at each other, so an "addu tera" (screen) is placed between them.

Kanyadanam

This is the ceremony where the bride's family gives her hand to the groom. The bride's parents wash the groom's feets considering the groom as the manifestation of Lord Vishnu who has come to marry their daughter who is considered as goddess Lakshmi.

Jeelakarra Bellam - Muhurtam

At the "Muhurtam" (auspicious time), Jellakarra Bellam is where the couple places a paste made from cumin seeds and jaggery on each other's head. This ceremony is observed to communicate that the married couple's relationship is unbreakable and they are inseparable. The "addu tera" (screen) is removed after this step and the bride and groom see each other.

Mangalayadharana

The groom ties the string of Mangalasutram (holy thread), with two golden discs, around the bride's nech. The Mangalasutram represents the physical, mental, and spiritual union of the couple.

Mattelu

In this ritual, the groom adorns the second toe of the bride with a silver toe ring. This is followed by the groom adorning the bride with a necklace of gold and black beads called "Nalla pusalu" in Telugu. Nalla pusalu is meant to free the bride from the effects of the evil eye.

Exchange Garlands

The bride and groom exchange garlands of flowers and those witnessing this occasion come forward to bless the couple by sprinkling flower petals.

Talambralu

This is the fun part of the wedding which not only the couple but the entire family enjoys. In this ritual, the bride and groom pour "Talambralu" (rice mixed with tumeric) on each other's head like a shower. The first 3 times, the couple shower "Talambralu" on each other properly, and after that it becomes a competition on who pouts more. Other things like rose petals, pearls, and colorful thermocol balls are also mixed in the rice.

Saptapadi

As part of the Saptapadi rituals, the groom and bride walk seven steps ("sapta meaning seven and "padi" meaning step) together, while they take seven vows of marriage.

Ungarala Binde

The bride and groom compete to be the first to find the ring in a pot of water. The winner of the game is said to have the upper hand in the marriage. This is another fun ritual that both sides of the family get involved in.

Arundathi and Eruvaka

Arundathi is a ritual where the bride and groom are shown the starets representing Arundathi and Vasistha. These stars represent the perfect couple complementing each other. For Eruvaka, the groom plants the cotton seeds as it is a tradition for farming families.

Aarati

The ceremony closes with the "Aarati" which involves the lighting a flame on a silver tray. The circular motions of the flame symbolize the removal of evil from the path of the couple.

Appaginthalu

This is when the bride traditionally joins the groom's family.
