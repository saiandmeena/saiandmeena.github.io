Rituals

All rituals performed in the marriage are described below.

Gauri Pooja

The bride offers her prayers to the goddess Gauri as she is the symbol of fertility and motherhood and prays for a similar ideal relationship that the goddess has with her husband, Lord Shiva.

Ganesh Pooja

All the auspicious occasions begin with a Ganesh Pooja since Lord Ganesha is believed to remove any obstacles in the way. Hence Ganesh Pooja is the first ritual that happens at the 'mandapam' (stage).

Vadhu Pravesam

The bride enters the 'mandapam' (stage). Until the 'muhurtam' (auspicious time), the bride and groom are not supposed to look at each other, so an 'addu terra' (screen) is placed between them.

Kanyadanam

This is the ceremony where the bride's family gives her hand to the groom. The bride's parents wash the groom's feet considering the groom as teh manifestation of Lord Vishnu who has come to marry their daughter who is considered as goddess Lakshmi.

Jeelakarra Bellam

At the 'Muhurtam' (auspicious time), Jeelakarra Bellam is where the couple places a paste made from cumin seeds and jaggery on each other's head. This ceremony is observed to communicate that the married couple's relationship is unbreakable and they are inseparable. THe 'addu terra' (screen) is removed after this step and the bride and groom see each other.

Mangalayadharana

The groom ties the string of Mangalasūtram (holy thread), with two golden discs, around the bride's neck. The Mangalasūtram represents the physical, mental, and spiritual union of the couple.

Mattelu

In this ritual, the groom adorns the second toe of the bride with a silver toe ring. This is followed by the groom adorning the bride with a necklace of gold and black beads called 'Nalla pusalu' in Telugu. Nalla pusalu is meant to free the bride from the effects of the evil eye.

Exchange Garlands

The bride and groom exchange garlands of flowers and those witnessing this occassion come forward to bless the couple by sprinkling flower petals.

Talambralu

This is the fun part of the wedding which not only the couple but the entire family enjoys. In this ritual, the bride and groom pour 'Talambralu' (rice mixed with tumeric) on each other's head like a shower. The first 3 times, the couple shower 'Talambralu' on each other properly, and after that it becomes a competition on who pours more. Other things like rose petals, pearls, and colorful thermocol balls are also mixed with rice.

Saptapadi

As part of the Saptapadi rituals, the groom and bride walk seven steps ('sapta' meaning seven and 'padi' meaning step) together, while they take seven vows of marriage.

Ungarala Binde

The bride and groom compete to be the first to find the ring in a pot of water. The winner of the game is said to have the upper hand in the marriage. This is another fun ritual that both sides of the family get involved in.

Arundathi and Eruvaka

Arundathi is a ritual where the bride and groom are shown the stars representing Arundathi and Vasistha . These stars represent the perfect couple complementing each other. For Eruvaka, the groom plants cotton seeds as it is a tradition for farming families.

Aarati

The ceremony closes with the 'Aarati' which involves lighting a flame on a silver tray. The circular motions of the flame symbolize the removal of evil from the path of the couple.

Appaginthalu

This is when the bride traditionally joins the groom's family.
